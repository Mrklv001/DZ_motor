Rising Risk of Fire or Explosion in Electric Motors
With the large-scale upgrading of industrial production, the application scenarios of electric motors have become increasingly complex. Harsh environments such as high temperature, high pressure, dust, and flammable & explosive media have made the increased risk of fire or explosion hazards a core challenge for many enterprises. Issues including equipment aging, insulation damage, and variable frequency out-of-control can trigger fires or explosions, resulting in huge financial losses and safety accidents – this is also the top concern for enterprises when purchasing electric motors.

Dazhong Core Electric Motor Series – Balancing Safety and High Efficiency

1. Y-H Series Marine Three-Phase Asynchronous Motor – Adapted to Harsh Marine Scenarios
Dazhong’s Y-H series marine three-phase asynchronous motor is custom-designed for marine applications. Adopting a fully enclosed self-fan cooling structure, it features high energy efficiency, water & moisture resistance, shock and vibration resistance, etc. It is suitable for driving marine pumps, ventilators and other equipment. Equipped with a basic fire and explosion-proof structure, it adapts to special marine environments such as condensation mist and oil mist, ensuring safe and stable motor operation during marine navigation.

2. YGP Series Rolling Mill Variable Frequency Speed Control Three-Phase Asynchronous Motor – Supporting Efficient Operation in the Metallurgical Industry
For the needs of the metallurgical industry, Dazhong has launched the YGP series rolling mill variable frequency speed control three-phase asynchronous motor. It features strong overload capacity, wide speed regulation range and stable operation, suitable for metallurgical roller table driving. It can be matched with various mainstream variable frequency devices at home and abroad to achieve precise speed regulation, balancing high-efficiency production and energy conservation. Combined with fire and explosion-proof inspection services, it eliminates potential safety hazards in high-temperature metallurgical scenarios.
