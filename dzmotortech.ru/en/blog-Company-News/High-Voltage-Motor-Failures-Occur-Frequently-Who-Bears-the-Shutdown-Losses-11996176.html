High Voltage Motor Failures Occur Frequently, Who Bears the Shutdown Losses?

  • Tuesday, 14 April 2026
  • 0
  • 52
  • 0
In continuous industrial production, once a high-voltage motor shuts down, it directly causes production capacity loss, rising maintenance costs, and increased safety risks. As a professional Chinese supplier, Dazhong Electric Motor Tech reduces shutdown risks for global users with stable and reliable high-voltage motors.

I. Three Fatal Failures of High-Voltage Motors: The Real Troubles Behind Production Shutdowns

1.1 Insulation Breakdown: The "No.1 Killer" of High-Voltage Systems

Moisture, dust, overvoltage, and long-term high temperatures will quickly degrade the insulation layer, causing inter-turn short circuits and ground faults, which directly lead to motor burnout and full-line shutdown. Under harsh working conditions, the insulation life of ordinary motors is greatly shortened, and hidden dangers are difficult to predict in advance.

1.2 Unstable Explosion-Proof Performance: Safety Red Line in High-Risk Scenarios

Scenarios such as oil and gas, chemical industry, and mining have extremely high requirements for explosion-proof structure and sealing. Ordinary high-voltage motors are prone to excessive gap in the explosion-proof surface, seal failure, and uneven heat dissipation, which cause sparks and overheating, posing serious safety hazards.

1.3 Low Energy Efficiency: "Invisible Loss" in Long-Term Operation

Inefficient motors have high losses and fast temperature rise, which not only push up electricity bills but also accelerate insulation aging, forming a vicious cycle of "failure - shutdown - maintenance". The long-term use cost is far higher than the purchase cost.

II. Who Bears the Shutdown Losses? The Unbearable Weight for Enterprises

- Production line shutdown: Hourly production suspension causes hundreds of thousands to hundreds of thousands of yuan in output value loss

- Maintenance costs: High expenses for replacing windings, bearings, and insulation systems

- Safety risks: Explosion-proof failure may lead to major accidents such as fires and explosions

- Delivery default: Order delays result in double losses of reputation and fines

The root causes of failures are mostly inadequate selection, materials, processes, and operation and maintenance. Choosing reliable manufacturers to reduce failures from the source is the key to loss control.
