Permanent Magnet Synchronous Motors for New Energy Locomotives---Driving Rail Transit Oil-to-Electrification Transition

  • Tuesday, 07 April 2026
  • 0
  • 52
  • 0
As a professional manufacturer of permanent magnet synchronous motors and a trusted Chinese supplier, Dazhong Electric Motor Tech has years of experience in the R&D and production of high-efficiency motors. With high-quality permanent magnet synchronous motor products, exceptional energy efficiency, customized production capabilities and in-depth global partnership networks, we precisely meet the demands of green upgrading in rail transit, provide core power solutions for oil-to-electrification projects, and help the industry achieve clean, efficient and low-cost operational transformation.

I. Oil-to-Electrification in Rail Transit: An Inevitable Trend for Industrial Development

Traditional fuel-powered locomotives have long been vital for rail freight, short-distance passenger transport, as well as shunting operations in mining and port areas, playing an important role in industrial development. However, with the global advancement of green and low-carbon concepts and the upgrading of rail transit operational requirements, their inherent operational limitations have become increasingly prominent, making them incompatible with the high-quality development of the industry.

Powered by diesel engines, traditional fuel locomotives emit large amounts of harmful gases such as carbon dioxide and nitrogen oxides during operation, with prominent unorganized emissions. This runs counter to the national carbon peaking and carbon neutrality strategies and the vision of green transportation, failing to meet strict current environmental protection standards. Meanwhile, fuel consumption accounts for over 60% of total locomotive operating costs. Market fluctuations in oil prices directly affect corporate profitability, and the complex energy conversion process of fuel results in an overall utilization efficiency of less than 40%, leading to massive energy waste.

In terms of power output, traditional fuel locomotives adopt complex mechanical transmission structures, causing delayed power delivery during startup and speed changes. They accelerate slowly under heavy loads, struggling to meet efficient transportation needs — especially in shunting operations, where frequent starts and stops greatly reduce transport efficiency. In addition, core components such as engines and transmission systems are prone to wear and tear, requiring regular disassembly and maintenance. This not only leads to long downtime for overhaul but also incurs high labor and spare parts costs, increasing operational burdens. Furthermore, mechanical operation and fuel combustion generate high-frequency noise, which negatively impacts the surrounding environment and the working conditions of operators, failing to meet the all-round requirements for green upgrading of rail transit.

Against this backdrop, replacing fossil fuels with electricity to drive the oil-to-electrification transition has become an inevitable trend for the sustainable development of the industry. Permanent magnet synchronous motors, with their advantages of high efficiency, energy saving and reliability, have become the core power components for new energy locomotives.

II. High-Quality Permanent Magnet Synchronous Motors: Core Power Support for Rail Transit Oil-to-Electrification

As an experienced Chinese supplier, Dazhong Electric Motor Tech has developed a full range of permanent magnet synchronous motors for new energy locomotives relying on core R&D technologies and extensive manufacturing experience. Each of our five series is tailored to specific working conditions and scenarios, delivering outstanding performance to promote the effective implementation of oil-to-electrification and demonstrate our high-quality manufacturing strength.



2.1 TYJX (TYC) Self-Starting Permanent Magnet Synchronous Motor

The TYJX (TYC) self-starting permanent magnet synchronous motor is a core component designed for light and medium-load applications of new energy locomotives. It features frame sizes ranging from 200 to 315, pole numbers including 6, 8, 10, 12 and 16, power output from 7.5kW to 132kW, and voltage compatibility with 380V, 660V and 1140V. It achieves Grade 1 energy efficiency in accordance with GB30253 Table 1 and holds energy-saving certification, fully aligning with the energy-saving upgrading needs of rail transit.

The greatest advantage of this series lies in its excellent self-starting performance. It requires no auxiliary starting equipment, delivers high starting torque with low starting current, and effectively avoids impacts on the locomotive power supply system during startup. It is suitable for short-distance passenger locomotives and light shunting locomotives. With low temperature rise, low noise and high power factor during operation, it ensures stable power output. The rotor design eliminates copper loss, further improving energy utilization. When equipped on new energy locomotives, it effectively reduces energy consumption of light and medium-sized locomotives and minimizes energy waste. Its compact structure also makes it ideal for retrofitting existing light and medium fuel locomotives without major structural modifications, ensuring convenient and cost-effective upgrades.



2.2 TBYP1 Explosion-Proof Variable Frequency Permanent Magnet Synchronous Motor

The TBYP1 explosion-proof variable frequency permanent magnet synchronous motor is custom-built for new energy locomotives used in special explosion-proof environments such as mines and petroleum sites. It has frame sizes from H80 to H355, power ranging from 0.55kW to 315kW, and rated voltage compatible with 220V to 1140V. It meets Grade 1 energy efficiency standards specified in GB 30252-2013 for permanent magnet synchronous motors, while complying with relevant explosion-proof specifications, combining explosion protection and high energy efficiency.

This motor series must be used with vector frequency converters. It adopts an built-in permanent magnet rotor structure with certain salient pole characteristics. When paired with a frequency converter using maximum torque/current ratio control, it maintains high power factor across the entire speed regulation range. With no-load and operating current reduced and low copper loss, the rotor windings generate no induced current, eliminating rotor resistance and hysteresis loss and further enhancing motor efficiency.
Its flameproof design meets multiple grades including ExdI Mb and ExdIIAT4 Gb, adapting to harsh and flammable environments such as underground mines and petrochemical facilities. Suitable for mine shunting locomotives and petroleum transport locomotives, it addresses the high pollution and insufficient safety of fuel locomotives in special scenarios while cutting operating costs through high energy efficiency. In addition, its power and mounting dimensions conform to IEC standards and correspond to German DIN42673 standards, making it suitable for global rail transit equipment in special environments.



2.3 TPTYC Variable Speed Permanent Magnet Synchronous Motor

The TPTYC variable speed permanent magnet synchronous motor focuses on precise speed control for new energy locomotives, with rated speeds of 120rpm and 160rpm and efficiency no less than 95.5%. Integrated with drive systems and PLC logic control, it is equipped with an intelligent cooling system that automatically adjusts water supply based on locomotive operation, effectively dissipating heat and ensuring stable long-term performance.

This series uses variable frequency speed regulation technology to achieve accurate and wide-range speed control with fast response, adapting to startup, acceleration, deceleration and constant speed operation. It is especially ideal for shunting locomotives and short-distance passenger locomotives requiring frequent starts and stops. Replacing traditional asynchronous motor and reducer drive systems, it saves no less than 20% electricity. Stable power output without mechanical impact reduces component wear and extends locomotive service life. Suitable for rail transit in steel, chemical and transportation industries, it meets efficient transport needs, improves energy utilization and helps optimize operating costs after oil-to-electrification.



2.4 TYCPX Low-Speed Direct-Drive Permanent Magnet Synchronous Motor

The TYCPX low-speed direct-drive permanent magnet synchronous motor is engineered for heavy-duty, low-speed applications. It features frame sizes H355 to H560, power from 160kW to 1600kW, pole numbers 4 to 12 (customizable per special requirements), and rated voltage compatible with 3000V, 6000V and 10000V. It meets Grade 1 energy efficiency requirements in GB 30254, with a compact, lightweight structure and exceptional load capacity.

Adopting a low-speed direct-drive design, it eliminates the need for additional reducers and connects directly to locomotive wheelsets, reducing power transmission links and mechanical energy loss. It also avoids wear and noise caused by reduction devices, greatly improving operational reliability. It can be provided with explosion-proof grades such as ExdI Mb and ExdIIAT4 Gb or non-explosion-proof versions. Widely used in heavy-duty freight locomotives and port shunting locomotives, it handles heavy loads easily with strong low-speed overload capacity and stable operation. Its fully enclosed structure blocks dust and moisture, operating reliably in temperatures from -50℃ to 40℃ across different regions. It significantly reduces maintenance costs and energy consumption for heavy-duty locomotives after retrofitting.



2.5 TYP1 Grade 1 Energy Efficiency Variable Speed Permanent Magnet Synchronous Motor

The TYP1 Grade 1 energy efficiency variable speed permanent magnet synchronous motor is a universal high-efficiency model suitable for most new energy locomotive applications. It must be used with vector frequency converters and features an built-in permanent magnet rotor with certain salient pole torque. When matched with a frequency converter using maximum torque/current ratio control, it maintains high power factor throughout the speed range, with low no-load and operating current and minimal copper loss.

Its outstanding strength is superior energy efficiency. With permanent magnets embedded in the rotor, the rotor runs synchronously with the stator magnetic field during normal operation, producing no induced current in rotor windings and eliminating rotor resistance and hysteresis loss. The motor maintains high efficiency and power factor when operating at 35% to 120% load, delivering remarkable energy-saving effects.

With a simple and reliable structure, it fits medium and light-duty freight, passenger and general shunting locomotives. It can directly replace the power system of traditional fuel locomotives or be used in oil-to-electrification retrofits with low difficulty and short cycles. It helps rail transit operators quickly achieve energy conservation and consumption reduction, while operating with low noise and temperature rise for improved comfort, in line with the development of green rail transit.

IV. Value of a Chinese Supplier: Advancing Green Transformation of Rail Transit

4.1 Promoting Clean Energy Structure Upgrading

Permanent magnet synchronous motors replace fossil fuels with electricity, driving the shift to clean and low-carbon energy consumption in rail transit, reducing harmful emissions and supporting carbon peaking and neutrality goals. A single heavy-duty freight locomotive equipped with our motors can save over 2 million kWh of electricity and reduce more than 75 tons of carbon dioxide emissions annually, creating significant long-term environmental benefits.

4.2 Accelerating Equipment and Technology Upgrading

Replacing traditional fuel power with permanent magnet drive technology promotes the development of more efficient, intelligent and lightweight rail transit equipment, breaking through technical limitations of fuel locomotives and improving power performance, reliability and intelligence.

The adoption of permanent magnet synchronous motors eliminates complex transmission and excitation systems, simplifying the powertrain and reducing component wear. Combined with variable frequency speed regulation and intelligent cooling, it enables precise control and efficient operation, elevating rail transit equipment technology to a new level.

4.3 Optimizing Industrial Operational Models

The high energy efficiency of permanent magnet synchronous motors greatly cuts locomotive operating costs, reducing reliance on fuel and avoiding risks from oil price fluctuations. The simplified structure lowers maintenance difficulty and costs while improving operational efficiency and service life. This drives rail transit operations toward low-cost, high-reliability and high-efficiency models, enhancing the core competitiveness of operators.

FAQ Tips

1.Q: What types of locomotives are compatible with Dazhong's permanent magnet synchronous motors for retrofitting?

A: They are suitable for diesel freight locomotives, short-distance passenger locomotives, port and mine shunting locomotives. Our five series cover light, medium, heavy-duty and explosion-proof scenarios, with customized solutions available for specific models.

2.Q: What is the service life of permanent magnet synchronous motors?

A: Under normal working conditions, the designed service life exceeds 15 years with no major overhaul required throughout the life cycle. The TYCPX low-speed direct-drive series, with its robust structure, can last more than 18 years, far exceeding the service life of power components in traditional fuel locomotives.

3.Q: What is the cruising range after oil-to-electrification retrofitting?

A: With lithium iron phosphate batteries, 2 hours of charging supports 8 hours of operation, meeting daily shunting and short-distance transport. Large-capacity energy storage systems extend the range to over 12 hours for heavy-duty long-distance freight. Kinetic energy recovery during braking further improves endurance.

4.Q: Do you offer explosion-proof permanent magnet synchronous motors?

A: Yes. The TBYP1 series is a Grade 1 energy efficiency explosion-proof variable speed motor, meeting explosion-proof grades such as ExdI Mb and ExdIIAT4 Gb, specially designed for locomotives in flammable and explosive environments like mines and petroleum sites, complying with national explosion-proof safety standards.

5.Q: How long is the R&D cycle for customized motors?

A: Standard customization (power, size, voltage adjustment) takes 15–30 days. Complex customization (special explosion-proof requirements, ultra-high power, extreme environment adaptation) takes no more than 60 days. We prioritize efficiency to ensure rapid project implementation.

6.Q: Do permanent magnet synchronous motors require a dedicated power supply system?

A: No. They are compatible with existing DC or AC power supply systems for rail transit without large-scale infrastructure modification. Only simple debugging is needed for adaptation, greatly reducing overall retrofit costs.

7.Q: Does traction decrease after retrofitting?

A: On the contrary, permanent magnet synchronous motors have higher power density. Traction increases by more than 13% compared with traditional fuel engines of the same size. The TYCPX low-speed direct-drive series delivers even greater traction improvement, increasing load capacity and transport efficiency.

8.Q: What after-sales support is provided?

A: We offer a 1-year free warranty and lifelong maintenance service. Global service outlets provide local technical support and component replacement. Our 24/7 hotline ensures a fault response within 48 hours, with regular inspection guidance for stable operation.

9.Q: Iss maintenance of permanent magnet synchronous motors difficult?

A: Maintenance is extremely simple. With no vulnerable parts, traditional excitation structures or complex transmission components, only regular dust cleaning and wiring checks are needed without disassembly. Annual labor maintenance costs are reduced by about 300,000 RMB per locomotive compared with fuel locomotives, greatly easing operational burdens.

10.Q: How to establish a global partnership with your company?

A: You may contact us through official consultation, offline negotiation or email communication. We will provide tailored cooperation plans including agency, supporting supply and joint R&D to jointly promote the green upgrading of rail transit.
